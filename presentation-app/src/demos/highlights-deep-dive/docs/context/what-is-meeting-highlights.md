# Meeting Highlights: AI-Powered Video Recap for Teams Meetings

**Meeting Highlights** is a new Microsoft Teams feature that delivers a **dynamic, bite-sized video recap** of your meeting. It uses AI to generate a short highlights video (typically **3‚Äì5 minutes** long) that combines a narrated summary with actual clips from the meeting, **spotlighting the most important moments** while preserving the meeting's original tone and flow. In essence, it offers a quick, engaging way for users to catch up on key discussions and the overall *"vibe"* of a meeting **without having to watch the entire recording**.

## Why Meeting Highlights?  üìù **User Needs and Feature Purpose**

In today's fast-paced work environment, it's common to miss meetings due to conflicts, time zone differences, or other commitments. **Catching up** afterward can be challenging. Before Meeting Highlights, people often relied on time-consuming methods: watching the full recording, reading lengthy transcripts or notes, or asking colleagues for summaries. These methods have clear drawbacks:

*   **Time investment:** Meeting recordings can be long, and most people don't have time to watch an hour-long video or sift through pages of transcript. In fact, internal data shows that the typical watch time for recorded sessions is under 10 minutes. Users needed a more efficient way to get the gist.
*   **Finding what matters:** It's tedious to scrub through a recording for the important parts. Users risk sitting through irrelevant sections, which wastes time and tests patience.
*   **Lack of context and "feel":** Text summaries or transcripts alone might convey facts, but they **miss the meeting's tone, mood, and urgency**. It's hard to gauge how discussions unfolded, the emotions or emphasis behind key points, or the meeting's overall atmosphere from text alone.

**Meeting Highlights directly addresses these pain points.** It was conceived to make missed meetings easier to digest by providing an **engaging recap** that condenses the meeting to its essence. By **automatically selecting and stitching together the most relevant segments** of the meeting and adding a coherent voiceover narrative, the feature helps users:

*   **Catch up quickly** on the *key points* of a missed meeting without watching the whole thing.
*   **Triage effectively**: decide whether a meeting warrants deeper review. After watching the highlights, a user can judge if they need to invest time in the full recording or follow-up materials. Often, the highlights alone answer their questions, but if something piques their interest, they know exactly which part of the full meeting to jump to.

Crucially, Meeting Highlights preserves elements of the actual meeting (like the **speakers' voices, demos shown, or reactions**) in the recap. This gives the viewer context that pure text cannot provide ‚Äì you can **hear the emphasis** in a speaker's voice during a critical point and **see any visual content** or slides that were shown. Early user research found that *"the meeting vibe is necessary to understand the outcomes"* and visuals and reactions *"are valuable to understand full context"* when catching up. By including real excerpts, Highlights brings in that missing vibe and authenticity, so you *feel* more like you actually attended the meeting.

## üîé **How It Works for the User: Experience and Interaction**

From a user‚Äôs perspective, using Meeting Highlights is straightforward. When a Teams meeting has ended and a recording is available, eligible users can **view the Meeting Highlights video** as part of the meeting recap experience. Key aspects of the user experience include:

*   **Integrated Video Player:** The highlights appear as a playable video. For example, in the Microsoft 365 Copilot (BizChat) interface, if you ask *"Summarize the meeting I missed"* or use the catch-up command, the AI will provide a text summary and then present a **Highlights video player** at the bottom of the response. In the future, Teams will likely surface a "View Highlights" option on the meeting's recap page in Teams itself. Either way, you click *Play* on the highlights video to start your quick recap.
*   **AI Voiceover + Meeting Clips:** As the highlights video plays, you'll hear an **AI-generated narration** explaining the discussion, **while seeing actual snippets of the meeting**. For instance, the video might show a clip of a presenter's screen or a speaker talking during an important segment, and over that clip you hear a synthesized voice summarizing what's being discussed or emphasizing the key takeaway. The narration acts as connective tissue between clips, so even when the video jumps between different parts of the meeting, it still feels like a cohesive story. (The voiceover uses a standard neutral voice ‚Äì in internal testing it's an Azure Cognitive Services female voice named "Anna," not any real person's voice, so no personal voice data is used.)
*   **Original Audio & Video Snippets:** In addition to the narration, the highlights will also let you **hear some original audio** from the meeting itself and see the real video for those moments. These **extractive segments** tend to be the most insightful or impactful moments ‚Äì e.g. when a decision was made, or a key explanation was given. Hearing the actual voices of participants in those moments conveys the tone (was the team excited? concerned? decisive?) and adds authenticity to the recap. For example, if during the meeting someone made an important announcement with enthusiasm, a short clip of that announcement may be included so you catch the emotional nuance.
*   **Subtitles and Navigation:** The highlights player includes **subtitles** for both the narrated portions and any spoken dialogue in clips. This is helpful if you're watching without sound or want to reinforce understanding by reading. Users can also navigate between sections ‚Äì for instance, skipping to the *"next highlight"* or back to the previous one with a click. This way, if a particular part isn't of interest, you can jump ahead, or if something was important, you can re-watch it easily. The interface might show markers or a timeline for each highlighted segment, helping users identify different topics covered in the highlights.
*   **Length and Pace:** The entire highlights video is only a few minutes long by design. It **omits less important chatter and routine parts** of the meeting, focusing only on high-value content. This keeps the viewer's attention and respects their time. The AI voiceover segments ensure that even with these omissions, the recap **flows logically** ‚Äì you won't feel lost when the video skips ahead, because the narration will explain context if needed (e.g. "*After discussing the timeline, the team moved on to budget concerns‚Ä¶*"). This combination of brief original clips and summative narration markedly **shortens the playback time** compared to watching the full meeting.

Overall, the experience is somewhat like watching a well-edited "highlights reel" of a sports game ‚Äì but for a work meeting. You see the ‚Äúplays‚Äù that mattered most, with commentary that ties it together. If something in the highlights piques your interest, you can always delve into the full recording or transcript from that point. Otherwise, in just a few minutes you come away knowing the key outcomes and **feel** of the meeting.

**Example:** Imagine you missed an hour-long project kickoff meeting. With Meeting Highlights, you can simply play the highlights video. You might hear an opening narration like, ‚Äú*Project Falcon Kickoff ‚Äì In this meeting the team introduced the project goals and decided on key milestones.*‚Äù Then you might see a 30-second clip of the project manager‚Äôs screen while they outline the goals, accompanied by the AI voice summarizing any discussion around them. Next, it might jump to a clip of a lead engineer excitedly explaining a challenge (letting you actually hear that excitement), then a narrated segment summarizing the team‚Äôs decision on timelines, and so on. In about 4 minutes, you‚Äôve caught the major points ‚Äì what the project is, who‚Äôs doing what, what the timeline is ‚Äì and sensed the enthusiasm and concerns of the team. You can move on with that knowledge, or if you heard something intriguing (say, mention of a specific requirement), you know you might want to watch that part in full later.

## üöÄ **Availability, Platforms, and How to Access It**

**Meeting Highlights is currently in preview** within Microsoft and rolling out in stages. It is an **enterprise feature** (available to enterprise customers/users) and is being delivered as part of the Microsoft 365 Copilot intelligent productivity suite, rather than a consumer Teams feature. Here's the status and platform support as of now:

*   **Preview (Dogfood and Private Preview):** As of late 2025, Meeting Highlights has been deployed internally at Microsoft (in MSIT, our internal cloud) for testing. A private preview for select enterprise customers was targeted to start around Sept 2025, pending approvals. During this phase, the feature is only available to users with the proper license and in participating organizations. Inside Microsoft, many employees can already use it in their daily workflow via the Copilot "BizChat" interface.

*   **Licensing Requirements:** The feature currently requires a **Microsoft 365 Copilot license** (or a Teams Premium license that includes the Copilot capabilities). In fact, at least one attendee of the meeting must have a Copilot license for that meeting's highlights to be generated. And to view the highlights video, a user also needs access to Copilot (the highlights are shown in the Copilot chat experience under the "Work" tab). This ensures the feature is used by the intended audience (AI-enabled enterprise users) and ties into the Copilot service.

*   **Client Platforms:** The long-term goal is to support **all major Teams endpoints ‚Äì Windows, Mac, web, and mobile (iOS/Android)**. However, during the preview, not all clients are enabled. **Currently, Windows Desktop (the new Teams client)** is fully supported for generating and viewing Meeting Highlights. Support for Mac is in progress ‚Äì the team expects Mac support by end-of-year 2025. (Indeed, as of a few months ago, highlights were intentionally **disabled on Mac** clients in Copilot until the experience is ready.) Mobile support is still being planned and is **not yet available**. In the interim, mobile users may rely on desktop or potentially view highlights via a link in mobile browser if provided, but the optimized mobile experience will come later. The product team originally debated a mobile-first approach (since catching up on the go is a key scenario), but ultimately chose a "**desktop-first, then optimize for mobile**" strategy to ensure a solid experience before scaling to phones.

*   **Where to find it:** In the current internal implementation, the primary entry point is through **Microsoft 365 Copilot (BizChat)**. A user can ask Copilot about a past meeting ‚Äì e.g. "*What happened in the project X review meeting yesterday?*" ‚Äì and Copilot will answer with text and then show a "Meeting Highlights" video player if that meeting's recording is available. In the future, Teams will integrate highlights directly into the meeting recap UI. For example, after a meeting ends, if you open the meeting details (in Teams calendar or chat), alongside the recording, transcript, and summary, you might see a Highlights section. This integration is planned for **Teams Recap** and possibly within the Teams calendar experience. The Meeting Highlights feature is also part of the **"Stay on Top" proactive experiences** in M365 ‚Äì so the system might even suggest a highlight to you for important meetings you missed (surfaced in places like the Viva Insights digest or Outlook, in the future). However, those integrations are still under development. Initially, you have to actively request or open the highlights.

*   **Rollout and Future GA:** The feature is on track to reach **General Availability (GA)** for enterprise customers in the **first half of 2024** as part of the **Teams Premium** offering (which encompasses Copilot's meeting intelligence). GA timing is contingent on testing and capacity, but the team's roadmap in 2025 indicates a goal of broader release once private previews are successful. So if all goes well, customers with the appropriate licenses should start seeing Meeting Highlights in their Teams experience in CY2024 H1. (It will likely be marketed as part of the **Intelligent Recap** features in Teams/Office 365.)

**Note:** Meeting Highlights is *not* available to consumer Teams users or organizations without the Copilot/Premium license. It‚Äôs very much aligned with Microsoft‚Äôs AI-driven productivity push for enterprises, and at this time there‚Äôs no indication it will appear in free or standalone Teams versions.

## ‚ö†Ô∏è **Considerations, Limitations, and Accessibility**

While Meeting Highlights is a promising feature, users and administrators should be aware of a few **current limitations and ongoing improvements**:

*   **Recording is Required:** The feature can only generate highlights if the meeting was recorded (since it relies on the recording's audio/video and transcript). If a meeting isn't recorded, naturally no highlights video can be created. So to benefit from Highlights, organizations should encourage recording important meetings. Additionally, very large meetings (like all-hands with hundreds of attendees) or very long sessions might have some constraints ‚Äì extremely lengthy recordings could be challenging to summarize comprehensively. The AI will still attempt to pick the best segments, but it may not cover every topic if a meeting had too many to fit into a few minutes. Highlights are meant to cover the crucial moments, not to fully index everything that was said.

*   **Content Coverage:** By design, **Meeting Highlights will not capture every detail** of the meeting. If something wasn't deemed among the "most relevant parts" by the AI model, it won't appear in the highlights video. This means some minor points or side discussions will be omitted. Users should treat highlights as exactly that ‚Äì highlights ‚Äì and not a replacement for the full meeting when 100% completeness is needed. If you have a meeting with multiple distinct sections or topics, some sections might get only brief mention or be skipped. The product team has internally noted that *highlights are not intended for full indexing of a meeting*. In practice, this hasn't been a big issue; most users find that the highlights hit the major themes. But it's wise to be aware that if something doesn't show up in the highlight reel, it might have been considered lower priority by the summarization logic.

*   **Accuracy of AI Summaries:** The AI-generated narration is generally high-quality, but it is automated. **Occasional inaccuracies or imperfect phrasing can occur.** Microsoft includes a disclaimer in preview that AI-generated content **"may be incorrect"**, and users should double-check critical information. For example, if a specific number or date was mentioned in the meeting, the highlights voiceover should capture it correctly in most cases ‚Äì but there's a small chance it could misstate a figure or miss a nuance. As the underlying large language model (LLM) and processing algorithms are refined, accuracy continues to improve. During private preview, the team is closely monitoring summary quality. In user testing, the summaries have been well-received, but this is an area of continuous tuning.

*   **Language Support:** Currently, Meeting Highlights has primarily been developed and tested for **English-language meetings**. Meetings conducted in English (even with various accents) are supported. The AI transcription and summarization for other languages is on the roadmap but may not be fully available yet. The voiceover narrator ("Anna") is an English voice. If a meeting is in another language, the system might either default to English narration of translated content or, more likely, not generate highlights at all until multi-language support is added. The team is actively working on multi-language support ‚Äì they have identified that even small changes in the narration voice for different languages can significantly affect user experience, so they are proceeding carefully. Expect more language coverage as the feature matures, but in the near term English is the focus.

*   **Accessibility:** As a video-based feature, ensuring **accessibility** for all users is critical. Microsoft is making sure Meeting Highlights is usable by people with disabilities. For deaf or hard-of-hearing users, the inclusion of **subtitles** is a major plus ‚Äì all narrated content and spoken dialogue in highlights is accompanied by on-screen text. This means users can read everything that's said in the highlights, which addresses a key accessibility need. For users with visual impairments or those who rely on screen readers or keyboard navigation, work is ongoing. Early testing uncovered some issues with keyboard accessibility ‚Äì for example, a bug was identified where keyboard focus did not move correctly through interactive elements in the highlights player after using the tab key. This would impact users who cannot use a mouse. The **expected behavior** is that a keyboard user can tab through the video player controls (play/pause, skip, volume, etc.) and any other clickable links (like "see full recap") in a logical order. The team has acknowledged this and is actively fixing the **focus order** so that the Highlights feature will meet accessibility standards (Microsoft's internal bug report referenced this as a compliance issue with focus order, which is being addressed). Additionally, the design includes standard player controls (like the ability to turn captions on/off, use keyboard shortcuts, etc.), and they are working on supporting high-contrast modes and screen reader announcements for all controls. In summary, while a few **accessibility bugs** exist during preview, the goal is to have Highlights fully accessible by the time of general release. Users with disabilities should soon be able to benefit equally from the quick meeting recaps.

*   **Technical Constraints:** A few technical aspects are worth noting (even if the user doesn't directly see them). The generation of a highlights video currently happens **on-demand** ‚Äì when a user requests it. In early previews, it can take on the order of up to \~30‚Äì60 seconds of processing before the video is ready to play. This is because behind the scenes, the system is calling the AI model and assembling the video. The team is striving to reduce this latency. They have plans to eventually pre-generate highlights for some meetings (similar to how the standard text recap is precomputed after a meeting) once the pipeline is optimized. For now, users might notice a short delay ("Loading your highlights‚Ä¶") before playback begins. Also, highlights are currently stored as metadata and generated fresh for each query ‚Äì they are not yet saved permanently as a file attachment to the meeting (though the team may move toward saving them as a video artifact in the future for reuse). These details generally don't require user action, but they explain why a user might only see highlights when connected online and not as a downloadable video, etc., in preview.

*   **Feature Evolution and Legacy:** It's worth mentioning that **Meeting Highlights has evolved through iterations.** An earlier attempt at providing meeting highlight snippets was trialed in the past (as a part of a legacy Stream or Teams experiment), but that version was **discontinued** and removed from code when it didn't move forward. The concept was later revived and redesigned as part of the broader Copilot initiative, which greatly expanded its capabilities (leveraging GPT-4, etc.) and improved the integration. So if you heard that "meeting highlights" was turned off previously, that referred to an **older implementation**. The current Meeting Highlights feature discussed here is the modern incarnation, actively in development and testing as of 2025, and is slated for release. Microsoft re-invested in this idea because internal research showed strong demand for better meeting catch-up tools, and advances in AI made the new approach far more viable. The ongoing development is a collaborative effort across teams (Teams, M365 AI, Stream/Clipchamp for video processing, etc.), ensuring that the feature works seamlessly within the meeting ecosystem.
